Predicting Soccer Match Outcomes

A Machine Learning Approach to Sports Forecasting

View Repository Run in Colab

Executive Summary

This project applies the complete predictive analytics workflow to forecast the outcomes of professional soccer matches. By leveraging historical match statistics, team formations, and player performance metrics, the goal is to identify hidden patterns that dictate match success. The analysis compares traditional logistic regression against advanced Support Vector Machines to determine the most effective forecasting method.

Business Context & Objectives

Accurate sports forecasting holds significant value for team management, scouting, and the sports analytics industry. The primary objective is to build a robust model capable of predicting a win, loss, or draw, which can translate into actionable insights for tactical adjustments on the pitch.

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

The dataset comprises over 10,000 matches. Initial exploration revealed significant distributions in possession metrics and shots on target. Anomalies in missing player data were handled via median imputation.

Methodology & Modeling

Data preprocessing included scaling numerical features and one-hot encoding categorical variables like team formations. Two primary models were evaluated:

  • Logistic Regression: Established as the baseline model to capture linear relationships between team stats and match outcomes.
  • Support Vector Machines (SVM): Deployed to capture complex, non-linear interactions. By applying the Kernel Trick (specifically the Radial Basis Function kernel), the model effectively mapped team performance features into higher dimensions, allowing for cleaner separation between winning and losing profiles.

Results & Comparison

The SVM model equipped with the RBF kernel outperformed the baseline logistic regression, showcasing a 14% improvement in predicting away-team upsets. Hyperparameter tuning focused on optimizing the C and gamma parameters to prevent overfitting.

Business Insights & Ethics

Actionable Recommendations: The model identified midfield possession in the final third as the strongest predictor of a favorable outcome. Teams can utilize this finding to prioritize midfield acquisition and tactical ball retention strategies.

Ethical Considerations: While sports analytics is generally low-risk regarding personal harm, it is crucial to recognize that models trained on historical data may inherit historical biases, such as overvaluing teams with historically higher financial backing.
